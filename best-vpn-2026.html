FAQ
Common Questions

Is using a VPN legal?

Yes — in almost every country. VPNs are legal tools in the US, UK, EU, Canada, Australia, and most of the world. A small number of countries restrict or ban VPN use. A VPN does not make illegal activity legal.

Does a VPN slow down your internet?

Yes, but the impact depends on which VPN you use. Premium VPNs using modern protocols typically cause 5–15% speed reduction on nearby servers. Cheaper or overcrowded VPNs can cause 30–60% drops.

What is the best free VPN in 2026?

ProtonVPN's free tier is the only free VPN we genuinely recommend. It has no ads, no data logging, no bandwidth cap, and is run by the same team that built ProtonMail.

Can a VPN get you banned from Netflix?

Streaming services actively try to block VPN IP addresses, but premium VPNs constantly update their server pools. NordVPN, Surfshark, and ExpressVPN unblock Netflix US, BBC iPlayer, and Disney+ reliably.

Does a VPN make you completely anonymous?

No — and anyone who tells you otherwise is misleading you. A VPN hides your IP address from websites and your browsing activity from your ISP. It does not make you anonymous to logged-in services or stop browser fingerprinting.
