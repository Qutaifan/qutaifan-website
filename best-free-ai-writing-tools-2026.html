Detailed reviews: what each tool does best

Best for speed and brainstorming

1. ChatGPT Free

OpenAI's ChatGPT (running on GPT-4o-mini by default) is the best free starting point for high-volume drafting. The free tier does not require a credit card, and you can generate unlimited conversations and documents using the mini model. It is exceptionally fast and excels at brainstorming hooks, structuring blog outlines, and generating bulk ad copy variations.

✔ Fast & unlimited mini model drafts
✖ Text can sound generic without detailed prompting
Best for natural copy and flow

2. Claude Free

Anthropic's Claude 3.5 Sonnet is the strongest free model for generating natural, human-sounding text. Unlike other models that write in a rigid, recognizable AI style, Claude captures nuance and writes with a fluid flow. The free plan is excellent for drafting complete articles, emails, or stories. The catch is the dynamic rate limit, which resets every few hours depending on server demand.

✔ Human-like writing style and flow
✖ Dynamic message caps during peak hours
Best for paraphrasing

3. QuillBot

QuillBot is a dedicated AI paraphrasing tool that lets you paste sentences and reword them instantly. The free web tier does not require signup and allows you to rewrite up to 125 words at a time. It is perfect for breaking up awkward phrasing, rewriting research notes, and polishing headlines. The free version restricts you to "Standard" and "Fluency" modes, but it is highly functional for quick edits.

✔ No signup required for quick paraphrasing
✖ 125-word limit per paste on free plan
Best for basic corrections

4. Grammarly Free

Grammarly is the industry standard for catching basic spelling and grammar mistakes as you write. The free plan offers unlimited spelling, grammar, and punctuation checks across browsers, desktop apps, and mobile keyboards. It is highly convenient because it integrates directly into Google Docs, emails, and CMS systems. Advanced suggestions (like tone, clarity, and vocabulary) are locked, but the free tier handles basic editing flawlessly.

✔ Great browser extension integration
✖ Advanced style suggestions locked in free
Best for editing readability

5. Hemingway Editor

Hemingway Editor is a local web app designed to make your writing bold and clear. It does not generate text; instead, it color-codes your draft to highlight sentences that are too complex, passive voice, and unnecessary adverbs. The online web version is completely free, unlimited, and does not require an account. It is the perfect tool to run your drafts through before publishing.

✔ 100% free web editor, no signup
✖ Does not generate drafts or auto-fix errors
Best for tone adjustment

6. DeepL Write

DeepL Write (from the creators of DeepL Translator) uses neural networks to improve the styling and tone of your sentences. The free web version lets you paste up to 2,000 characters at a time. It suggests alternative vocabulary, fixes grammatical structure, and offers tone presets (e.g. professional, casual). It is exceptionally good for non-native speakers looking to sound natural in English or German.

✔ Excellent tone adjustments
✖ 2,000 character limit per check

Free-tier limits you should know

AI writing assistants rely on expensive server computations, which is why free tiers always have constraints. Understanding these limits prevents disruptions to your workflow:

  • Character/Word Caps: Paraphrasing tools like QuillBot (125 words) and DeepL Write (2,000 characters) restrict how much text you can process at once. Break your articles into paragraphs to fit these limits.
  • Rate Limits: General chatbots (specifically Claude) restrict how many questions you can ask in a given timeframe. Having Claude and ChatGPT open side-by-side lets you switch to ChatGPT if Claude hits a rate limit.
  • Locked Features: Editors like Grammarly restrict clarity and vocabulary improvements on their free plans. Hemingway web app is free, but its offline desktop app is a paid purchase. Use the web app for free editing.

Pro-Tip: Never put a credit card down for simple draft writing. Use ChatGPT and Claude free tiers first. Keep your prompts detailed and specific to get the best structure, and use Hemingway's web app to edit for readability.

FAQ: Free AI writing tools in 2026

Do free AI writing tools own my data?
Most cloud providers (including OpenAI and Anthropic) may use free-tier conversations to train their AI models unless you opt out. Avoid pasting sensitive personal data, corporate secrets, or proprietary code into free web interfaces. Check settings to turn off training history.
Is QuillBot free version good enough for academic writing?
Yes, the Standard paraphrasing mode on QuillBot Free is highly effective for rewriting clumsy sentences or adjusting structures. The 125-word limit requires you to paste paragraph-by-paragraph, but the core functionality is fully there.
What is the difference between Grammarly Free and Premium?
Grammarly Free corrects spelling, punctuation, and basic grammar errors. Grammarly Premium adds full-sentence rewrites, tone detection, vocabulary adjustments, and plagiarism checkers. For basic clean copy, the free version is completely sufficient.
