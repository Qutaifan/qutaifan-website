Buyer's Guide

How to Choose the Right Email Marketing Platform

Six factors that actually separate a good email platform from an expensive mistake.

1

Deliverability rates — the only metric that matters first

A perfectly designed email that lands in spam is worthless. Before comparing features, check third-party deliverability benchmarks for each platform. Industry average inbox placement is around 83%. Platforms like Mailchimp, MailerLite, and ActiveCampaign consistently hit 95%+. Low deliverability on a shared IP pool — often a problem with budget senders — silently kills your ROI regardless of how good your content is.

2

Automation depth — define your sequences before you sign up

Map out the automations you plan to build before evaluating platforms. A simple welcome sequence and weekly newsletter needs only basic automation (Mailchimp, MailerLite). A multi-step onboarding sequence with conditional branching based on user behavior requires ActiveCampaign-level capability. Paying for automation depth you'll never use is wasteful; underbuying and hitting a ceiling six months in is costly.

3

List size pricing — calculate your 12-month cost honestly

Most platforms charge based on subscriber count, and costs escalate faster than most people expect. A list that grows from 1,000 to 10,000 in a year can increase your monthly bill by 5–10x. Calculate costs at 1k, 5k, 10k, and 25k contacts before committing. Brevo's volume-based model can be dramatically cheaper if you have high contact counts but send infrequently. Klaviyo becomes expensive quickly as lists scale — factor this in for ecommerce stores with rapid growth.

4

A/B testing capabilities — essential for improving performance

Subject line A/B testing is table stakes — all platforms offer it. The differentiator is what else you can test: email body content, send time, CTA placement, sender name, and full automation path variants. ActiveCampaign and Klaviyo offer the deepest A/B testing. MailerLite includes subject line and send time testing on paid plans. Mailchimp's multivariate testing requires Standard tier or above. If systematic conversion optimization is a priority, verify testing depth before committing.

5

Integrations — check your stack before signing

Email marketing software doesn't operate in isolation. Verify native integrations with your CMS (WordPress, Webflow), ecommerce platform (Shopify, WooCommerce), CRM (HubSpot, Salesforce), and any tools you rely on for lead capture. Mailchimp leads with 300+ native integrations. Most others use Zapier or Make to bridge gaps, which adds cost and latency. Native integrations sync faster, break less, and don't require an additional subscription.

6

GDPR & compliance — non-negotiable if you have EU subscribers

Every platform on this list offers GDPR-compliant data processing agreements and unsubscribe mechanisms. The differences are in the details: double opt-in enforcement, consent timestamp logging, CCPA compliance for US senders, and data residency options (EU-based servers for stricter compliance requirements). If you operate in regulated industries or serve EU customers, verify the platform's DPA terms and data processing infrastructure before importing your list. Brevo and ActiveCampaign have the most mature compliance documentation.

FAQ

Common Questions, Answered Honestly

What is the ROI of email marketing in 2026?

Email marketing consistently delivers the highest ROI of any digital marketing channel — approximately $36 for every $1 spent, according to the Data & Marketing Association's most recent benchmarks. Compare that to social media ($2.80), paid search ($2), or display advertising ($0.80). The reason is simple: your email list is an owned asset you control entirely, with no algorithm changes or platform fees reducing your reach. A list of 10,000 engaged subscribers is worth more than 100,000 social followers on a platform you don't own. The ROI is highest for ecommerce (where revenue can be directly attributed to campaigns) and lowest for businesses with long, complex sales cycles where email is one touchpoint among many.

Mailchimp vs ConvertKit — which is better in 2026?

They serve different audiences and the "better" depends entirely on what you're building. Mailchimp is better for: product-based businesses, ecommerce (with caveats — Klaviyo beats both for serious stores), businesses needing rich visual email templates, and teams wanting the widest integration ecosystem. ConvertKit is better for: newsletter-first creators, bloggers, podcasters, course sellers, anyone building a personal brand audience, and anyone who wants to sell digital products directly through their email platform. If your emails look like a brand marketing piece, choose Mailchimp. If your emails read like a personal letter from a person, choose ConvertKit. The free plans are both generous (500 contacts for Mailchimp, 1,000 for ConvertKit), so test both before committing.

What is the best free email marketing platform?

MailerLite offers the most capable free plan: 1,000 subscribers, 12,000 emails per month, automation workflows, landing pages, pop-ups, and newsletter monetization — all at no cost. ConvertKit's free plan is close behind (1,000 subscribers, unlimited sends) but has fewer template options. Mailchimp's free plan is more limited than it used to be (500 contacts, 1,000 sends/month) but the brand recognition and 300+ integrations make it worth considering. Brevo's free plan allows unlimited contacts but limits sends to 300/day — ideal for large lists that send infrequently. For most small newsletter operations just starting out, MailerLite's free plan is the most generous starting point in 2026.

How can I improve my email open rates?

Open rates in 2026 are influenced by five factors in order of impact: (1) Sender reputation — your domain must have proper SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records configured, or Gmail and Outlook will quietly route your emails to spam. (2) Subject line — test 2–3 variants; the biggest wins come from curiosity gaps, specificity, and personalization (first name in subject line still works). (3) Send time — test Tuesday–Thursday mornings in your audience's time zone as a starting baseline, then refine with your platform's send-time optimization. (4) List hygiene — remove unengaged subscribers (no opens in 90+ days) quarterly; a smaller, active list outperforms a bloated stale one. (5) Consistency — audiences open from senders they recognize and trust. An irregular cadence trains people to ignore your emails. Note: Apple MPP has inflated open rate metrics since 2021; track click-through rate and revenue per email as your primary performance indicators.

What email marketing features actually matter for automation?

For basic automation (most small businesses): trigger-based emails (sign-up welcome, birthday, anniversary), multi-email sequences with time delays, and simple conditional splits (clicked link → send follow-up). Mailchimp, MailerLite, and ConvertKit handle all of this cleanly. For intermediate automation (growing businesses): behavior-based triggers (visited a pricing page, watched a webinar, abandoned cart), lead scoring, and A/B split paths within automation workflows. GetResponse and Klaviyo cover this well. For advanced automation (B2B, SaaS, large ecommerce): multi-branch conditional logic based on CRM data, site tracking, integration with sales pipeline stages, and cross-channel coordination (email + SMS in one workflow). This is ActiveCampaign and Drip territory. Buy the tier that matches where you are today, not where you aspire to be in three years — you can always migrate when you've grown into it.
