Buyer's Guide

How to Choose the Right AI Meeting Assistant

Use this decision framework before paying for another SaaS subscription.

1

Decide if a visible bot is acceptable

If a bot joining the call changes what clients, executives, or investors say, start with Granola. If not, Fathom, Fireflies, Otter, and tl;dv are easier to standardize across teams.

2

Pick personal notes or system-of-record sync

Fathom is excellent for personal productivity. Fireflies and Avoma are stronger when meeting outputs need to land in Salesforce, HubSpot, or a manager's coaching workflow.

3

Match platform coverage to your calendar

If you only use Zoom, Fathom's free tier is hard to beat. If meetings span Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams, compare Fireflies and tl;dv before committing.

4

Test degraded audio, not demo calls

Run each tool on a real call with accents, background noise, and technical vocabulary. Clear-audio demos hide the failures that matter in production.

5

Calculate team cost at scale

A $10/user plan becomes meaningful at 20 seats. Before rolling out company-wide, decide who truly needs recording, who only needs shared notes, and who can stay on free.

6

Review recording and consent policy

AI meeting assistants can create compliance obligations. Confirm local recording rules, client expectations, and enterprise security requirements before using any notetaker externally.

FAQ

Common Questions, Answered Honestly

What is the best AI meeting assistant in 2026?

Fathom is the best default pick for most individuals and small teams because of its generous free tier, fast summaries, and low setup friction. Fireflies.ai is stronger for sales teams that need CRM sync. Granola is best when you need bot-free notes on sensitive calls.

Which AI meeting assistant is best for sales teams?

Fireflies.ai is the strongest sales-team pick for most buyers because it emphasizes CRM integration, cross-meeting search, action items, and multilingual support. Avoma is worth considering when managers need coaching scorecards and deeper revenue-intelligence workflows.

Is there an AI meeting assistant without a bot?

Yes. Granola is the leading bot-free option for Mac users because it captures audio locally rather than sending a visible bot into the meeting. That makes it useful for client calls, executive conversations, and advisory work where bot presence creates friction.

Are free AI meeting notetakers good enough?

Often, yes. Fathom and tl;dv both offer useful free workflows, and Otter has a limited free tier. Paid plans become more compelling when you need team administration, CRM integrations, longer retention, custom templates, or more meeting volume.

Should I worry about recording consent?

Yes. Any meeting recording or transcription tool can trigger legal, compliance, or client-trust concerns. Use clear consent practices, follow local laws, and avoid recording sensitive calls until your organization has an approved policy.
